Hello! My name is Dominic Maggio and I'm a PhD candidate at MIT working on spatial scene understanding for robotics focusing on Open-Set Semantic Mapping, Monocular Localization, and 3D Scene Representations. I'm advised by Luca Carlone in the SPARK Lab. I obtained double major bachelor degrees in Aerospace Engineering and in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at MIT in 2021 with a 5.0/5.0 GPA and a Masters in Aerospace Engineering from MIT in 2023.

Research

I'm working on the following problems at the intersection of robotics and computer vision:
  • Form Open-Set 3D Semantic maps at the correct granulatity (i.e., what is an object) to support an agents tasks.
  • Combining semantic information across multiple views to get an overall semantic understanding of the scene.
  • Monocular camera pose estimation with lightweight memory and computation usage.
  • Creating new efficient spatial and memory models of the 3D scene for both localization and semantic understanding.
